State Assistance (Insurance) Program

The State Assurance (Insurance Program), known as SAP, is a State Coverage insurance plan designated to all residence of selected states. These states will get no Out-of-Pocket repair costs for areas damaged by accidents, environmental conditions, or otherwise destruction caused by criminals. Allowing the structures around your area to be left in pristine condition as if nothing ever happened.

Coverage is instanly established when you:

  • Gain a State ID/license
  • Purchase a property
  • Become a government employee of any level
  • Establish a Construction site with safety fencing or gates surrounding the area
  • Establish a factory with fencing/walls
  • Declare of a vehicle to the Department of Transport/Motor Vehicles
  • Declare your town, city, or metropolitan area as a high upkeep area
  • Lord a Land used for the purpose of short/long-term rent, or medical facility establishment for the otherwise enabled (Landlord)
  • and a general set of additions made, depending on your local state laws and coverage regulations...

Coverage is not covered under the pretense of:

  • Visitation of the state
  • Planning of property purchase, but not under the law of contract
  • Construction Equipment, or Construction buildings that're not inhabital for long-term capabilities
  • Vehicles that're found in a conflict zone, or conflicting area with potentially life-threatening events
  • Vehicles that're currently stored with sensitive storage entities
  • Trees, or other landscape items that're found in the wilderness and not in the disturbance of motorists/bystanders
  • Renters, medical dependants, or residence that're not able to prove their assistance in household upkeep
  • Some exceptions may also occur depending on the conditions of the affected area, conflicts surrounding the repair site, or other regulations that both ensure the safety of the State and SmartRepair Agents.

Unsure if your property is covered? Contact your Real Estate Agent, Personal Home Insurer, or contact your State Area Manager found on the Staff page above.

Texas FAIR Plan Association and Home Insurance Guides

The Texas FAIR Plan, established in 1995, was set in the works to review parties that were denied by other coverage providers. Allowing process of gaining insurance for properties without the hassle of other private companies, and instead gaining a State provided insurance plan. These plans are by default set with the SAP in all contracted plans.


You'll also find other guides and assistance tools to help you review your potential coverages in the state of Texas. If you believe that SAP isn't being added to your plan, ask the insurer that it was already included and to define what areas are covered for your home. Including additions and other pieces of property that're expected to be covered.

Department of Financial Services New York

Under the Department of Financial Services for the state of New York, all policies are bounded with SAP. Even on HO-1, Basic Homeowners Policy, contracts. Failure of application for SAP will only be defined by the insurer and not the affected parties defined by a Policy Holder. Internal components of a residence that may be covered by HO-2+ policies will not be reviewed by SmartRepair Agents, and only external items like fencing, lighting, and other external components can be placed for repair under the SAP.
